About Me

Hello, my name is Maxwell Mitchell. I'm a Mechanical Engineering student at Washington University in St. Louis with a passion for building, designing, and understanding the physical mechanics of the world around us.

I am from Waterloo, Illinois. Waterloo, Illinois is a small town of about 10,000 people located 45 minutes outside of St. Louis, Missouri. Growing up in a small town, I learned to always be hands on. Additionally, living in Waterloo has instilled a stron sense of community and work ethic that I use every day today. Growing up in my house hold, we had plenty of animals I was able to tend to (dogs, cats, horses, chickens, etc.). For my first job I worked at a local farm where I was able to branch two of the things I love the most - working with my hands and caring for animals. While working on the farm during weekends and summers, I also was involved in many different sports, finding another passion with football. Football has futher helped me develope my sense of becoming a leader and pursuing bigger an better goals. I worked at the same farm and continued playing football from middle school through my senior year of high school. Finishing high school left me with the big question of what do I want to do with my life . . .

While finishing my senior year of high school, I was able to visit schools with my parents to look for my best fit academically and athletically. Fortunately, I was accepted into Washington Univeristy in Saint Louis (WashU) where I was given the opportunity to do both. Coming into WashU at first was a big adjustment. I had never been used to being around so many people; however, I quickly grew to love it. Additionally, the workload for being a Mechanical Engineering student came fast but I was fortunate to build a strong foundation of friends who I could collaborate and work with.

One of the issues I encountered with the adjustment was my physical health. The years of playing football had caught up to me and I had to make one of the hardest decisions of my life. After three knee surgeries, I decided to hang up the cleats. What started as a sour moment planted the idea of a new passion. Music and playing the guitar had always been an interest of mine growing up. When I entered my sophomore year at WashU, I moved into a new apartment called "Syth" with my friends. Together we all had a passion for music and would jam in our basement on random nights. Eventually, random nights turned into every night while we learned and wrote songs. Today we know have a fully developed band and play show around the St. Louis area. The best part about it has been the ability to express myself in a new creative outlet. Not only that, but Syth has allowed us to create an emotional outlet for WashU students, staff members, family, friends, and many more important people. Being able to reahc out to a community and develope relationships with all of these people has been one of the most rewarding experiences of my life.
